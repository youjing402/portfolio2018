Journey Builder Smart Testing

Redesigning the testing feature of Salesforce Journey Builder to boost marketers' confidence.

Overview

In this client-sponsored project, we, a team of 4, collaborated with UX designers and researchers from Salesforce to improve and re-imagine the user experience of one of their most successful digital marketing tool: Journey Builder.

As marketing campaigns become more complex and personalized, marketers have to deal with more uncertainty, because a small mistake can lead to frustration of a large audience.

The challenge now is: how do we increase marketers' confidence when they activate a marketing campaign in Journey Builder?

Contribution Highlights

Through the use of mapping tools, understood the service and user journey to identify problems systematically.

Designed interfaces and interaction based on Salesforce design system, product features, and product goals.

Communicated with clients and users to iterate on design ideas and prototypes.

Solution Overview

In short, we chose to focus on redesigning the journey testing experience. We made testing more systematic and efficient by adding in the following features:

Testing Progress Tracking

To ensure easy and systematic testing, a progress tracking system was introduced. Test cases can be automatically generated to test uncovered paths in the journey.

In-App Test Message Preview

Instead of sending messages to different email accounts and going through each of them, marketers can now check the message content in local testing results, and mark the test case if anything goes wrong.

Research

Challenge 1 - Understanding Users and Workflow

What does "confidence" mean to marketers?

In the exploration stage, we tried to understand marketers' goals and general experience with building campaigns. In doing so, we were searching for things that are associated with markters' confidence.

What did we do?

User Research & Interview

As a team, we intereviewed 3 marketers to learn about their experience in marketing campaigns. We also did online research to learn about detailed Journey Builder use cases.

Competitive Analysis

To learn about current trends and technologies, we collected over 50 relevant products in the market and highlighted their advantages.

Experience & Mental Model Map

We synthesized research results by mapping out marketers‚Äô tasks, tools used, emotions, and pain points throughout different stages of a marketing campaign.

Salesforce Marketing Cloud Research

To help us better understand how marketers navigate through the Marketing Cloud ecosystem to achive theier goals, we conducted further Salesforce product research.

What did we learn?

1. Factors that influence marketers' confidence

What marketers are trying to do can be summarized into 3 core elements: Making sure to send the right message to the right people at the right time. Other than that, they also have to deal with other external factors, such as collaboration, software complexity, KPI, etc.

Their gain confidence mainly from gaining sense of control: know what they want to achieve, what they can achieve, and what they have achieved.

2. Marketers' confidence fluctuation throughout a campaign

Markers' confidence dropped significantly when they went into the campaign execution stage because (1) Unlike the highly collaborative pre-execution stage, marketers now need to take sole responsibility during the execution stage; (2) More uncertainty and variants are introduced during the actual execution.

Pain Points: Zooming into the experience

Why do marketers feel insecure right before activating campaigns?

We conducted further interviews with 2 marketers regarding their feelings and experience right before campaign activation and learned that marketers' biggest insecurity comes from not being in control.

Specifically, it becomes harder to make sure everything is accurate, and more stressful to think about the consequences caused by small mistakes:

Mistakes can be Scaled up

Even one small mistake on the journey canvas can lead to thousands of real users receiving the wrong message, which causes exponential amount of anxiety.

Journey can Grow Crazy

A journey can easily become huge and complex with tens and hundreds of branches. The fearsome of missing out small details adds to marketers' insecurity.

Pain Points: Zooming into the product

Why did the existing testing feature fail to address such insecurity?

Following the line, we took a closer look at the testing feature and marked relevant pain points at each step.

What did we learn?

Uncertain about the final look of messages

Marketers are only able to see the message template in content editor, and there's no way to check how messages will end up be like on users' device during testing unless markters' send test messages to real email accounts.

Tests are independent and not trackable

Currently, each test is independent from each other, and markters can only test up to 10 contacts at a time, which can become tedious if the journey grows huge. Also, it's hard for marketers to keep track of overall testing progress without the help of external tools.

Design

Design Goals

To tackle pain points that we identified in the current design, we came up with a high-level feature concept and defined design goals accordingly:

1. Systematic Testing System: To maintain a trackable testing system that helps marketers test journey thoroughly and gain more control over the testing process.

2. Test Result Preview: To make it more efficient for marketers to check journey logics and message content.

[Mechanism]

Systematic: How to ensure everything is tested in an efficient manner?

1. OVERALL MECHANISM

üëé Before: Contact-First Approach is inefficient, tedious, and risky

A journey typically has many branches, and different contacts will go through different path according to their attribute configuration. Thus, to ensure test coverage, marketers now have to configure, select and record contacts carefully on their own.

üëç After: Path-First Approach helps marketers focus on more important tasks

We decided to make this process more automated by focusing on the path coverage: marketers only have to take care of which paths they want to test, and the system will pick the right group of contacts to cover those paths.

2. TEST CASE GENERATION

Testing & Iterations

Iteration 1: Regrouping UI elements

1. According to users, the placement of prev/next button was not intuitive. We therefore re-examined buttons in the bottom navigation bar, and replaced unnecessary ones with prev/next buttons. It became neater and more logical.

2. Switching between status was inflexible and confusing through text. We designed a dropdown that enabled users to easily switch between different status.

Iteration 2: Changing the style of path event panel

1. In the first version, we tried to be more consistent with the visual design in journey editor, and thus designed panel in the style of timeline.

2. However, users were confused which event was selected in the panel, as the highlight was not clear enough.

3. We thus replaced the timeline with buttons.

Final Results

Impact & Measurement

Reflection

  • Don't start with interfaces: Designers from Salesforce didn't encourage us to look into interface details when we first started off on the project. We were confused and panicked at first, but soon realized that by doing so, we stopped thinking about trivial UI improvements. This helped us focus more on users' general experience and dig out different areas they felt insecure about, and we were therefore able to bring deeper insights into the interface design stage.
  • Product compatibility and design system: Unlike crafting new products, working on existing ones require designers to be well aware of the design guidlines and conventions established in the original product. In this project, we worked with Salesforce's Lightning Design System. Although it limited our creativity of creating fancy interactions, we felt more secure when making design decisions, as there's always something to rely on when we're indecisive.
