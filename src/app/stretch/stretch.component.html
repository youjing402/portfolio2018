Problem Space

Food insecurity is defined as a lack of consistent access to enough food for an active, healthy life. 1 in 7 people struggles with food insecurity in Indiana, and the problem could be more prevalent than we thought. People are not always aware of the risk, and they may not have the skills to prepare for the future risk or handle it when it comes.

Food insecurity is often a temporary state, and everyone can be at risk at some points in his or her life."
-- Coordinator at Community Kitchen, a local NPO combatting food insecurity in Bloomington, Indiana


How can we prevent the risk of food insecurity by helping people to plan meals and manage food in ways that make a better use of budget and time?

Design Outcome

We came up with, Stretch, a meal planner that features the key concept of "stretching" meals.

Plan meals through multiple days

Be in control of your budget and time

Budget and time are two keys to meal preparation. Users can plan several meals ahead based on available budget, time, and food to gain an overview over effective resource usages.

Easy, quick, flexible recipes that stretch your meals

Save and reallocate time and energy: prepare extra and repurpose for future meals

Holistic recipe planning features the core concept of "stretching the food". Based on users' cooking availabity, they will be instructed to prepare more food when they have time, and the extra portions will be reused or directly consumed in future meals to save time and make better use of ingredients.

Manage your grocery shopping and food stock

Use what you have, get what you need

Users can shop for all grocery items that are required by the planned meals at once with the easy-manage grocery list. Also, users can check through the quantity and expiry dates of food stock to decide what to use in next meals and improve food usage efficiency.

Track your budget, spending, and time saved

Gain overview of your progress and resources, and see your improvements over time

Planning meals is rewarding and motivating, particularly when the progress becomes visible: how you save your time and budget, and how you better utilize your resources. Based on the statistics, users will be able to better manage and adjust their resources.

Budget, Time, and Life Uncertainty

Understanding Food Insecurity as a Temporary State

We had the assumption that people confronting food insecurity are living in extreme poverty. But the result from our field research disproved this: People going for cheap food are just normal people who work hard to feed themselves and their family.

Food insecurity is not as simple as 0 and 1; due to life uncertainties, anyone could .

Often, as a result of life uncertainties, budget and time becomes challenging, and planning for meal preparation to stay food secure becomes crucial yet harder. This led us to the question that:

"When it comes to meal preparation under budget and time constraints, how can we help individuals become more proactive and resilient to potential risks of food insecurity?"

Problems Identification

How do people prepare meals and manage their food

To learn about issues people have in meal preparation that can be potentially improved, we interviewed 20 people about their eating habits, meal preparation, and food management process.

Based on the interviews, we mapped out people's journey of meal preparation in different stages: from planning, shopping, preparing, to consuming. We synthesized and analyzed their experience, problems, and opportunities for improvement.

Synthesizing Insights

What hinders people from making the best use of resources

People want to make the best use of their budget and time, but they are not because they don't have the awareness and skills to plan and prepare meals in a holistic way:

People generally lack the time, skills, and awareness to plan meals ahead, and are unsure of what comes next.

Many do not have the habit of planning grocery list, ingredients, and individual meals. They do see the value of planning, but they find it tedious and do not worth the time and energy. Without a plan, they over-spend the budget and time, and they do not have a clear picture of what is coming.

People have limited knowledge about efficient usages of ingredients across meals.

People are used to choosing from a limited range of ingredients they are familiar with. Also, many would not consider the various usages of ingredients, despite that it can be helpful when planning efficient food use across different meals.

People are used to cooking just enough, yet they complain about making meals from scratch when they don't have time.

Making every meal from scratch is time consuming and energy consuming. Sometimes people would just eat processed food or skip meals for convenience. However, people often do not cook more than needed, mainly due to lack of awareness.

Inspiration

Learning from Experts

How Successful Individuals and Organizations Deal with Food

To further learn about professional skills of meal planning and food management that could benefit people, we looked into organizations and a fitness trainer who are experts at food management and meal preparation, to see what practices and strategies help them make efficient use of resources.

Affinity Diagram

We used affinity diagram to organized and synthesized findings we gathered from research with experts and organizations. Through analyzing and summarizing the categories, we learnt about their effective food practices.

Story Mapping

We mapped out the flow that experts manage food, plan meals, and prepare meals. In that, we considered alternative scenarios in different stages and compare with average people's story to find design opportunities.

Oppotunities for Application

Key Inspirations

Plan and do with a big picture in mind

They planned in advance, and for many meals across days at once. Keeping the big picture allowed them to plan the most suitable recipes, adjust ingredients in different meals, reuse for better utilization, and allocate time and energy.

When actually making meals, they often prepared more than needed: to save time for future meals, and to avoid accidents.

Maintain food organization

Keeping food in good organization allowed them to track food freshness and quantity, which greatly helped them with food usage planning: the right ingredients with right quantity to be consumed on the right day.

Not only raw ingredients, leftovers were also a big part in food management for efficient reuse.

Ideation

What-If Scenarios

Based on what we learned about people's pain points in meal preparation and management, we jumped out of data and brainstormed multiple concepts from coming up with what-if scenarios.

Semantic Differential

We grouped concepts into 3 clusters, discussed the pros and cons by considering previous what-if scenarios, and performed semantic differential to analyze their feasibility and level of impact.

Through assessing the feasibility and impact of different concepts, we chose two of the most promising ones and saw the potential of combining them together:

Envision

What if meals are pre-planned and cooking time is re-allocated based on what one has?

+

(1) Personal Meal Planner:
Make a better use of money by planning meals throughout the week based on one's food budget.

(2) Stretch One's Food:
Make a better use of food and time by cooking more at once and utilize the extra portion to prepare the next meal(s).

We wanted to design a meal planner that teaches people how to plan their meals ahead and resuse food smartly in the way that make a better use of their resources.

Conceptual Testing

Validating the Core

To test out the concept in a quick and dirty fashion, we wanted to know (1) Whether in practice, such planning method achieves the goal this product aims for: to save resources and to make people feel safe and in control as we expected, (2) What thoughts and problems people may have when following the plan.

Experience Prototyping

We created a concept prototype by designing a 4-meal experimental recipe with the help from the expert and asking participants to follow the 2-day instructions from grocery shopping to cooking.

Paper Prototyping

We also created a paper prototype capturing the key flow from planning meals, getting grocery items, to making meals. We tested with participants to learn about their quick reactions about the key idea and flow.

Feedback and Iteration

Design for More Rewarding and Flexible Plan-Following Experience

Effective saving of resources

Participants agreed that planning left-over repurposing and preparing extra for future is very helpful in saving resources, particularly time.

Sense of Security

Planning multiple days ahead, getting all ingredients, and preparing extra portions help user stay away from worries about uncertainties.

Planning could be troublesome

It is hard to be determined to start a new plan, and it is stressful to stick to it, unless the perceived benefit is not big enough to outweight such inconvenience.

Allowing flexibility is crucial

Since meals are dependent, the planner should be more flexible to handle cases like missing a meal or changes of ingredient stock.

The idea of planning for stretching meals was proven to be worth-developing, and that we needed to take care of making it easy and rewarding to plan and follow plans, and maintaining a fair level of flexibility in the planning mechanism.

Final Design

Design Highlights

1) Flexible cooking time arrangement based on users' availability

Time is one of the huge concerns. Therefore, we considered the design where preparation from scratch is no longer necessary for every meal: based on one's availability of cooking, meals can be arranged to fit into one's schedule and save time. Stretching food happens in 2 forms: repurpousing food prepared in previous meals to make new meals, and directly eating the extra-prepared meal.

2) Flexible adjustment towards planned meals in case of changes

To ensure flexibility in order to lower the barrier for users to follow plans, adjustments are allowed to be made on planned meals. When the user is no longer able to prepare a meal, he/she can choose to postpone, skip, and edit it. Depending on cases, plan could be kept the same, or modified for resource optimization.

3) Smart ingredients planning in improvement of food usage efficiency

To improve food usage efficiency, the user will be recommended to use food he/she already has in the pantry, and meal plans containing the ingredients will be generated to ensure food is consumed before expiry, and that food usage is efficient.

Reflection

  • Be flexible: There is no such thing as universal design process: sometimes instead of sticking to a design cookbook, diving into research insights and be flexible enough to switch between directions is much more important.
  • And, be flexible: In different stages of design, designer's mindset is expected to change and adjust: sometimes from top-down view, sometimes from down-top view, sometimes diverging, sometimes converging, sometimes focusing on details, sometimes focusing on overview. It is very important to identify the right strategy in different situations, and be open-minded to jump forwards and backwards.
